Angular

I have worked professionaly with AngularJs for 3 years now. I know it extensivly. I enjoy its flexability and vastness. It is a very powerful library. I have helped create three distict and separate web applications on on the back of AngularJs. We have incuded dozens of intigrated modules, hundres of services and directives, and massive extendability which has kept our code very DRY. With all of these it was ever necessary to handle the communication to the backend Api. Dozens of xhr reqests and state mangment. The sites are hight traffic, receiving hundreds of thousands of visits per month and continue to be proformant.

Angular, or Angular2 as it is often called (or 4, it gets confusing) is the next itteration of AngularJs. I have had the chance to learn directing from Angular and Google team members about this library. While my hands on experience is much less, at its core, its stil Angular. I know how to dive into sourcecode and into documentation to quickly learn how a library is suppsoe to work and get it functioning.
